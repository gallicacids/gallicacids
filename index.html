What is Gallic Acid?

Gallic acid is a type of phenolic acid commonly found in various plants, fruits, and teas. It is known for its antioxidant, antimicrobial, and anti-inflammatory properties, making it a popular compound in health and medicine.

Sources of Gallic Acid

Gallic acid is widely found in plants like gallnuts, sumac, witch hazel, tea leaves, oak bark, and various fruits like blueberries, apples, and strawberries.

Benefits of Gallic Acid

  • Antioxidant properties help protect cells from damage.
  • Anti-inflammatory effects can help reduce swelling and pain.
  • May have anti-cancer properties by inducing apoptosis in cancer cells.
  • Used in traditional medicine for its antibacterial and antiviral effects.
